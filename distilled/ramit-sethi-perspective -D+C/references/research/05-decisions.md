# Ramit Sethi Research 05: Decisions

## Decision Pattern 1: He deliberately chose aspiration over deprivation

### Evidence

- About page
- Conscious Spending Basics
- Episode 254

Ramit consistently rejects "don't buy lattes" finance culture and replaces it with Rich Life language, guilt-free spending, and higher-order questions.

### Inference

This is not a rhetorical flourish. It is a brand and philosophical decision.

Confidence: High

## Decision Pattern 2: He chose psychology as the explanatory engine

### Evidence

- About page
- Money Scripts page

He explicitly says money psychology and invisible scripts are what hold people back more than tactics alone.

### Inference

He intentionally differentiated himself from tactical-only finance educators.

Confidence: High

## Decision Pattern 3: He chose automation as the operating system

### Evidence

- Conscious Spending Basics
- About page
- Money Dials
- Episode 254

### Inference

He sees automation not merely as convenience but as the backbone of reliable behavior change and reduced conflict.

Confidence: High

## Decision Pattern 4: He chose live couples coaching as a public teaching format

### Evidence

- Podcast index page
- Episodes 255 and 257
- couples finance articles

### Inference

This lets him demonstrate that money problems are relational, emotional, and behavioral in real time. It is both educational and differentiating.

Confidence: High

## Decision Pattern 5: He chose to make big wins emotionally compelling

### Evidence

- About page: $30,000 questions vs $3 questions
- Episode 254: six-step wealth path and anti-optimization framing
- couples materials: fixed costs, account structure, automation

### Inference

He is not only teaching leverage. He is packaging leverage in memorable, psychologically sticky language.

Confidence: High

## Decision Pattern 6: He chose to normalize wanting more

### Evidence

- About page: no limit to how much you can earn
- About page: money can support adventure, generosity, spontaneity
- Episode 254: encourages people to describe what rich feels like in concrete terms

### Inference

He deliberately removes moral shame from ambition and spending, then tries to reconnect those desires to systems and trade-offs.

Confidence: High

## What these decisions imply for the final skill

The final skill should behave like someone who:

- refuses guilt-based finance advice
- asks what a Rich Life looks like before optimizing
- pushes systems before discipline
- finds hidden scripts before prescribing tactics
- treats couple conflict as a financial design problem, not a personality flaw
