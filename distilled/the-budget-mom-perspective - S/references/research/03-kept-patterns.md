# 03 Kept Patterns

## Keep / Why / New Use

### Pattern 1: Receive emotion before giving guidance

Keep:

- start with what the person is feeling

Why:

- people listen better when they do not feel misread

New use:

- content hooks
- comment replies
- discovery chats
- hesitant-prospect replies

### Pattern 2: Separate identity from behavior

Keep:

- treat the problem as a fixable pattern, not a character flaw

Why:

- this reduces shame and defensiveness

New use:

- when prospects feel "late," "behind," "confused," or "bad at money"

### Pattern 3: Reframe before recommending

Keep:

- change the lens before proposing the next step

Why:

- reframing creates psychological room for action

New use:

- move from fear to clarity
- move from avoidance to readiness
- move from "I don't know" to "let's look at it properly"

### Pattern 4: Keep the next step small and contained

Keep:

- do not ask for ten moves at once

Why:

- small next steps feel safer and more believable

New use:

- invite a review
- invite a conversation
- invite a simple check-in
- invite a first planning step

### Pattern 5: Build trust with steadiness, not dominance

Keep:

- calm confidence
- soft certainty
- low-drama structure

Why:

- S-style trust rises when the advisor feels steady rather than forceful

New use:

- premium advisory branding
- long-horizon client relationships
- sensitive conversations around protection, planning, and uncertainty

### Pattern 6: Use values-and-life framing instead of pure math framing

Keep:

- connect choices to peace, clarity, values, and life fit

Why:

- people do not buy because numbers are correct; they buy when the choice feels right and safe

New use:

- help prospects understand why a conversation matters
- connect advice to peace of mind, readiness, protection, or long-term confidence

## Summary

The S-style layer worth keeping is not a product philosophy.
It is a trust sequence:

- receive
- reduce shame
- reframe
- steady
- invite
