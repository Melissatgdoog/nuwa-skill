# 04 Excluded Ideology

## Why This File Exists

The source corpus is useful for communication study, but not all of its worldview should carry into the final skill.

The user explicitly does not want:

- a frugality-first identity
- a budget-coach identity
- a save-more / cut-more public brand
- messaging that repels people looking for protection, planning, or premium advisory help

## Remove These Source Tendencies

### 1. Budgeting as the center of all financial change

Do not carry forward the idea that the main answer to money stress is a better budget.

Why to exclude:

- too narrow
- too retail
- too close to a budgeting influencer identity

### 2. Saving and spending reduction as the default corrective move

Do not carry forward the tendency to solve pressure by:

- cutting
- pausing purchases
- wanting less
- simplifying consumption as the primary virtue

Why to exclude:

- it pushes the final voice toward scarcity-coded advice
- it creates the wrong audience magnet

### 3. Frugality as moral cleanliness

Do not carry forward any frame where restraint becomes a moral badge.

Why to exclude:

- it shrinks the user's brand
- it attracts the wrong expectations
- it makes the advisor sound like they are coaching denial rather than designing security or confidence

### 4. Budget compliance as proof of seriousness

Do not carry forward the feeling that "good" clients are disciplined savers and "bad" clients are spenders.

Why to exclude:

- it reintroduces shame
- it damages advisory trust
- it reduces room for premium, future-oriented positioning

## Replace With These Frames

- from budgeting -> planning
- from cutting back -> making wiser decisions
- from restraint -> readiness
- from compliance -> clarity
- from guilt -> responsibility
- from less -> better aligned
- from deprive yourself -> protect what matters

## Red Flags During Writing

If the draft starts sounding like any of these, it has drifted too close to the source ideology:

- "spend less"
- "cut unnecessary expenses"
- "want less"
- "just stick to the plan"
- "discipline is the answer"
- "you need to stop buying things"

These can appear in the research, but they should not become the final skill's center of gravity.
