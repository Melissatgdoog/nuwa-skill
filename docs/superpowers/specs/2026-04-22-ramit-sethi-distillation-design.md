# Ramit Sethi Distillation Design

## Goal

Create two artifacts inside `C:\github\nuwa-skill` for Ramit Sethi:

1. A directly usable character skill that captures Ramit's broad public thinking system.
2. A companion analysis document that explains how his ideas and communication methods work so the user can study and reuse them.

This is a full-spectrum distillation, not a narrow finance-only one. The output should cover:

- Rich Life philosophy
- conscious spending and automation
- money psychology and invisible scripts
- high-leverage financial decisions
- couples and partner money conversations
- Ramit's media and persuasion style

## Scope

### In scope

- Distill Ramit from high-confidence public materials, prioritizing official sources.
- Focus on how he thinks, teaches, persuades, and reframes money problems.
- Build a skill useful for:
  - analyzing personal finance decisions in Ramit's style
  - rewriting money advice with Ramit's framing
  - handling partner money conversations with Ramit-like scripts
  - producing Ramit-style content hooks and explanations
- Write a study-oriented analysis document that separates transferable methods from persona-specific branding.

### Out of scope

- Reconstructing his full biography beyond what is supported by primary sources
- Treating low-confidence secondary summaries as core evidence
- Writing anything outside `C:\github\nuwa-skill`
- Reducing Ramit to generic budgeting advice

## Approach Options Considered

### Option 1: Content-style-first

Focus on his hooks, delivery, and media charisma.

Pros:

- immediately useful for content mimicry

Cons:

- too shallow for a full Ramit distillation

### Option 2: Money-framework-first

Focus on Rich Life, conscious spending, money scripts, and couples systems.

Pros:

- strongest philosophical extraction

Cons:

- undercaptures his media and persuasion method

### Option 3: Dual-layer full-spectrum distillation

Build a skill that combines framework plus expression, then use a separate analysis document to explain the system.

Pros:

- best fit for the user's request for a comprehensive version
- preserves both utility and teachability
- avoids collapsing Ramit into either pure style or pure finance theory

Cons:

- synthesis is more complex

Recommended option: Option 3.

## Extraction Hypotheses

Current hypotheses to verify from the source set:

- Ramit's real unit of analysis is not "budgeting" but "designing a Rich Life."
- He consistently attacks small, guilt-based money advice in favor of high-leverage moves.
- He treats automation as a liberation system, not just an efficiency tool.
- He sees money conflict in couples as emotional and relational, not merely mathematical.
- He often diagnoses invisible scripts and identity stories before giving tactics.
- His persuasion style is direct, unembarrassed about desire, and comfortable challenging shame.

These only become core skill models if they are well supported across multiple official sources.

## Testing Strategy

The final skill should be mentally checked against prompts like:

- "How would Ramit respond to someone obsessed with cutting coffee but avoiding salary negotiation?"
- "How would Ramit coach a couple who avoids talking about money?"
- "How would Ramit explain guilt-free spending without sounding irresponsible?"
- "How would Ramit rewrite a fear-based finance script into a Rich Life script?"

Success criteria:

- output sounds like Ramit rather than generic finance advice
- it balances aspiration and practicality
- it includes systems, psychology, and clear action
- couples advice sounds relational, not merely numerical

## Risks And Mitigations

- Risk: overusing secondary coverage.
  - Mitigation: keep official site and official podcast pages as the center of gravity.
- Risk: flattening Ramit into "spend extravagantly" slogans.
  - Mitigation: pair spending philosophy with automation, systems, and priorities.
- Risk: underrepresenting couples work.
  - Mitigation: ensure partner money conversations are a major section, not a side note.
- Risk: copying surface bravado without underlying structure.
  - Mitigation: separate expression DNA from deeper mental models.
