# Humphrey Yang Distillation Design

## Goal

Create a full Humphrey Yang distillation package inside `C:\github\nuwa-skill` that does two things at once:

1. Produces a directly usable perspective skill.
2. Produces a companion analysis document that explains how his communication and teaching system works.

This should be a full-spectrum distillation, but with deliberate extra weight on the part of Humphrey Yang's public style that feels high-I:

- infectious and socially shareable
- story-guided rather than lecture-heavy
- easy to retell
- emotionally inviting without becoming confessional
- concrete, friendly, and hook-driven

The user also asked for a strict constraint: remove Humphrey Yang's self-introduction patterns, name announcements, and overly personal autobiography-style storytelling from the final distilled voice.

## Scope

### In scope

- Distill Humphrey Yang from public materials, prioritizing official or first-party channels where possible.
- Capture his money-education frameworks, audience calibration, simplification methods, and content packaging habits.
- Emphasize his I-type influence traits:
  - approachable hook construction
  - scenario-based explanation
  - conversational momentum
  - social-energy phrasing
  - memorable contrast and retellability
- Translate his public style into a reusable perspective that can:
  - explain money concepts simply
  - rewrite finance content into a more inviting and viral format
  - help the user study and borrow his persuasive storytelling methods

### Out of scope

- Writing biography-heavy material
- Retaining first-person self-branding or self-introduction habits
- Leaning on private-life or intimate life-story details as a primary support beam
- Writing anything outside `C:\github\nuwa-skill`

## Source Strategy

### Source priority

1. Humphrey Yang official website or official about pages, if available
2. Humphrey Yang official YouTube channel pages, transcripts, interviews, or official descriptions
3. First-party interviews or podcast appearances where his own phrasing and teaching style are visible
4. Limited external commentary only when it helps describe public reception and is clearly labeled

### Research target

The source set should be broad enough to support all six research files:

- writings and structured explanations
- long-form conversations
- expression DNA
- outside reception
- observable decisions about content packaging
- basic public timeline

### Source discipline

The final package should keep core claims grounded in first-party material. Any inferred communication principle should be labeled as an inference when it is not directly stated.

## Approach Options Considered

### Option 1: Pure finance-framework distillation

Pros:

- clean and structured

Cons:

- undercaptures the user's main request around I-type influence and storytelling

### Option 2: Pure communication-style distillation

Pros:

- strongest focus on voice and persuasion

Cons:

- too thin for a full-person distillation

### Option 3: Full distillation with expression-weighted rewrite

Pros:

- matches the user's "complete person distillation" request
- keeps the thinking system and the communication system together
- allows targeted removal of self-intro and personal-life material while preserving charisma

Cons:

- requires more careful separation between durable method and persona residue

Recommended option: Option 3.

## Extraction Hypotheses

Current hypotheses to verify from the source set:

- Humphrey Yang's public power comes from making finance feel like an easy social conversation instead of a technical lecture.
- A large part of his I-type influence comes from scene-setting and retellable contrast, not just from friendliness.
- He often leads with familiar situations, visible numbers, or "here's what people get wrong" pivots.
- His accessible finance style depends on simplifying without sounding patronizing.
- Some of his public charisma is attached to self-branding and personal positioning, but the underlying persuasive structure can be preserved after removing those parts.

These should only become final mental models if they remain visible across multiple source types.

## Testing Strategy

The final skill should feel strong on prompts like:

- "Rewrite this dry finance paragraph so it feels more engaging, social, and easy to retell."
- "Explain this investing idea to a beginner in a friendlier Humphrey-style way, without self-introduction."
- "Turn this money lesson into a story-shaped hook with concrete numbers."
- "What would this perspective say to someone who finds finance content intimidating or boring?"

Success criteria:

- the result sounds lively, welcoming, and socially transmissible
- the answer uses narrative motion rather than just bullet-point teaching
- the voice avoids "I am Humphrey" framing and avoids leaning on private anecdotes
- the underlying finance explanation still stays accurate and concrete

## Risks And Mitigations

- Risk: copying surface friendliness without the underlying structure.
  - Mitigation: extract hook logic, contrast logic, and scene-setting patterns explicitly.
- Risk: over-preserving personal-brand residue.
  - Mitigation: actively rewrite openings and persuasion moves away from self-reference and biography.
- Risk: making the result too generic after removing personal anecdotes.
  - Mitigation: preserve concrete scenario framing, numeric contrast, and social momentum.
- Risk: overusing external summaries.
  - Mitigation: keep first-party material as the center of gravity.
