# S-Style Finance Guide Distillation Design

## Goal

Create an anonymous perspective skill inside `C:\github\nuwa-skill` distilled from a public-facing money educator, with deliberate emphasis on S-style communication:

- steadiness
- emotional containment
- relational trust
- shame reduction
- gentle forward movement

The final output should feel usable as a communication operating system, not as a biography, fan summary, or persona imitation.

## Scope

### In scope

- Distill the speaker's public communication patterns from official materials.
- Keep only the parts that support calm, trust-building, non-judgmental money guidance.
- Produce:
  - an anonymized `SKILL.md`
  - a short anonymized analysis document
- Store the final distilled files under `distilled/`.

### Out of scope

- Personal biography
- Public milestones or career history
- Brand slogans, name-first intros, or signature watermark phrases
- Strongly combative or hype-first persuasion moves that are not needed for the S-style target

## Source Strategy

Primary sources:

- `https://herfirst100k.com/`
- `https://herfirst100k.com/about-us`
- official blog posts and official podcast/show-notes pages on `herfirst100k.com`

Research focus:

- how the speaker opens emotionally charged money topics
- how she normalizes shame, guilt, and overwhelm
- how she reframes identity away from blame
- how she moves from empathy into action without becoming cold or preachy

## Extraction Hypotheses

The source set suggests the final skill should preserve these patterns:

- begin with recognition of the user's lived feeling, not immediate correction
- separate behavior problems from identity shame
- frame money as a tool, not a moral verdict
- replace deprivation language with permission, alignment, and values
- offer one manageable next step rather than a hard push

The final skill should remove these patterns:

- source-person self-introduction
- source-person career story
- source-person branded slogans and signature naming
- source-person autobiographical proof moves

## Risks And Mitigations

- Risk: the result becomes too generic after removing the source persona.
  - Mitigation: preserve concrete sequencing, sentence habits, and trust-building moves.
- Risk: keeping the source's stronger activist or hype-first energy overwhelms the S-style target.
  - Mitigation: include only what supports warmth, steadiness, and safety.
- Risk: analysis drifts into biography.
  - Mitigation: explicitly describe communication mechanics instead of life history.
